Our Projects

This project aims to provide a simple and effective way to help farmers detect and manage citrus diseases in orange plants. It focuses on identifying five of the infectious diseases that affect the Nepali orange market - black spot, canker, Huanglongbing (HLB), Leaf miner and sooty mold along with recognizing healthy leaves. With the use of Mobilenetv2 model in a mobile app to upload images of plants, farmers receive disease predictions, presented in an easy-to-understand format with confidence levels shown as bar graphs and pie charts. The app also includes a Llama-3.2 RAG based chatbot model that specializes in citrus-related topics, helping farmers identify diseases by analyzing leaf images and offering practical advice on prevention, treatment, and management of orchards. Beyond disease detection, the system provides a valuable resource for farmers to learn about best practices in orange cultivation and help them make informed decisions to protect their orchards. By combining advanced technology with a farmer-friendly design, this project empowers orange farmers to monitor plant health more effectively, improve productivity, and install knowledge driven practices over unsourced techniques ensure sustainable farming practices for a healthier orchard.
